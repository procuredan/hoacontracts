HOA Replacement Cost Analysis

Replacement cost analysis helps determine whether an HOA’s insurance policy reflects the true cost to rebuild buildings after a major loss.

Many associations unknowingly carry outdated property values that no longer match current labor costs, material pricing, and regional construction conditions.

At HOA Contracts, we help HOA and condo associations review replacement cost exposure before insurance renewals lock communities into another term.

Why Replacement Cost Matters

If replacement values are inaccurate, associations may face major financial exposure after claims.

  • Underinsured property exposure
  • Coinsurance penalties
  • Large special assessments
  • Delayed rebuilding timelines
  • Reserve account strain
  • Unexpected owner costs after claims

Accurate replacement cost calculations are one of the most important parts of an HOA insurance review.

Common Problems Associations Face

During insurance reviews, we frequently see outdated valuations that no longer reflect today’s rebuilding environment.

  • Replacement values several years out of date
  • Rapid increases in local construction costs
  • Policies based on outdated market conditions
  • Missing code-upgrade considerations
  • Incorrect assumptions about reconstruction pricing
  • Insured values that do not reflect current exposure

Construction Costs Continue To Rise

Labor shortages, inflation, material pricing, and regional rebuilding demand continue to affect construction costs across many states.

Insurance values that were appropriate several years ago may no longer provide adequate protection today.

Many boards do not realize they may be underinsured until after a claim occurs.

What A Replacement Cost Review Includes

A replacement cost review may evaluate:

  • Current insured building values
  • Square footage assumptions
  • Roofing and exterior exposure
  • Common structures and amenities
  • Local labor and material trends
  • Inflation guard adjustments
  • Ordinance & law requirements

Why Boards Review Before Renewal

Insurance renewal season is one of the best opportunities to evaluate whether current replacement values still reflect real-world rebuilding costs.

Competitive insurance reviews may also identify alternative deductible structures, carrier options, or policy enhancements that better align with the association’s needs.
