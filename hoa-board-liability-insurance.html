HOA Board Liability & D&O Insurance

HOA board members make important financial and operational decisions on behalf of their communities. Directors & Officers insurance helps protect boards from certain claims related to those decisions.

What Is HOA Board Liability Insurance?

Directors & Officers (D&O) insurance helps protect board members and associations against certain claims involving governance and operational decisions.

Coverage structures vary by carrier and policy, but may include protection related to:

  • Governance decisions
  • Fiduciary responsibility
  • Financial management
  • Rule enforcement disputes
  • Vendor and contract decisions
  • Discrimination-related allegations
  • Employment-related claims

Why Boards Review Liability Coverage

HOA boards are increasingly aware of governance exposure, litigation costs, and insurance-related liability concerns.

Many associations have not reviewed board liability protections in years, even as operational and legal risks continue to evolve.

  • Coverage exclusions
  • Outdated liability limits
  • Carrier-specific restrictions
  • Changing litigation environments
  • Reserve and assessment concerns

Common Issues We See During Reviews

During HOA insurance reviews, boards often discover liability structures they did not fully understand.

  • Low liability limits
  • Outdated D&O policy structures
  • Missing endorsements
  • Coverage exclusions boards were unaware of
  • Policies that no longer match the association’s operational risk
  • Gaps between property coverage and board protections

Why Insurance Renewals Matter

Insurance renewal season is an opportunity to evaluate whether the current carrier and policy structure still align with the community’s needs.

Competitive insurance reviews may identify:

  • Improved liability structures
  • Better board protections
  • Alternative deductible options
  • Carrier differences in D&O coverage
  • Potential premium savings opportunities
