Why HOA Insurance Costs Are Increasing

Many HOA and condo associations are seeing higher insurance premiums, larger deductibles, and more difficult renewal terms. In many cases, boards are surprised by increases because the insurance program has not been reviewed or competitively evaluated in years.

A proper HOA insurance review can help your association understand what is driving the increase, whether the current coverage still fits the property, and whether better options may exist.

Common Reasons HOA Insurance Premiums Go Up

Insurance premiums can increase for many reasons. Some are driven by broader market conditions, while others are specific to the association’s property, claims history, or coverage structure.

  • Rising property replacement costs
  • Higher claim frequency across the insurance market
  • Weather-related losses and regional risk factors
  • Older buildings or deferred maintenance concerns
  • Outdated coverage limits or policy structures
  • Limited carrier competition for association risks

Why HOA Boards Should Not Simply Renew Without Review

Many associations renew the same insurance policy year after year because it feels easier than shopping the market. But automatic renewals can lead to missed savings opportunities, coverage gaps, or outdated terms.

Even when the current carrier remains the best option, a review gives the board clarity. It helps confirm whether the premium is reasonable, whether the coverage is appropriate, and whether there are better alternatives available.

Signs Your HOA Insurance May Need A Review

  • Your premium increased significantly at renewal
  • Your deductible changed or increased
  • Your board has not reviewed insurance in 3+ years
  • Your property values or replacement costs have changed
  • You have had recent claims or carrier concerns
  • Your association is unsure whether coverage limits are still accurate

Insurance Savings Should Not Mean Less Protection

The goal is not just to find a cheaper policy. The goal is to make sure your HOA has appropriate coverage, competitive pricing, and a clear understanding of its risk.

How An HOA Insurance Review Helps

An HOA insurance review can help identify whether the association is overpaying, underinsured, or simply working with outdated information. It can also help boards prepare for renewal before they are forced into a rushed decision.

  • Review current coverage and deductibles
  • Compare market options when available
  • Identify possible coverage gaps
  • Evaluate whether current premiums are competitive
  • Help the board understand next steps before renewal
