What Is An HOA Cable Agreement?

An HOA cable agreement is a contract between an association and a service provider for cable, internet, or related telecommunications services. Depending on the property, the agreement may cover bulk service for residents, access rights, infrastructure, marketing rights, or revenue-sharing terms.

Common Types Of HOA Internet & Cable Agreements

  • Bulk service agreements where residents receive service through the association
  • Access agreements allowing providers to serve the community
  • Revenue-sharing agreements that may provide income to the association
  • Marketing agreements giving a provider preferred access or visibility
  • Infrastructure agreements involving wiring, equipment, or upgrades

Why These Agreements Matter

Cable and internet agreements can affect monthly dues, resident satisfaction, vendor access, technology upgrades, and long-term association flexibility. If the agreement is outdated, the HOA may be missing better pricing, better service, or potential revenue opportunities.

Important Review Point

Many agreements include renewal terms or notice deadlines. If the board waits too long, the contract may automatically renew before better options are evaluated.

What HOA Boards Should Review

  • Current pricing and service levels
  • Resident complaints or service issues
  • Contract renewal dates and notice deadlines
  • Provider access rights
  • Revenue-sharing or compensation terms
  • Upgrade obligations or infrastructure limitations
  • Whether competing providers can offer better options

Signs Your HOA Cable Agreement May Be Outdated

  • The agreement has not been reviewed in several years
  • Residents complain about speed, reliability, or service options
  • The contract renews automatically
  • The association is unsure whether it receives revenue or other benefits
  • New providers are available in the area
  • Pricing no longer appears competitive
