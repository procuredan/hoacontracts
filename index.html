Where Most HOAs Lose Money

Most HOA boards assume their vendor contracts are competitive. In reality, many associations quietly overpay year after year because contracts renew automatically, vendors increase pricing, and no one benchmarks agreements against current market options.

  • Internet & cable agreements with outdated pricing or missed revenue opportunities
  • Landscaping contracts with price creep and weak performance standards
  • Trash and waste services with oversized service levels or poor bidding
  • Maintenance vendors that have not been competitively reviewed
  • Insurance policies that may be overpriced, outdated, or underinsured

The 5 Contracts Most HOAs Overpay On

  1. Cable & internet — bulk agreements, access rights, and revenue programs
  2. Landscaping & grounds maintenance — recurring contracts that often rise over time
  3. Trash & recycling — service levels and pricing that may not match actual need
  4. Maintenance & equipment service — elevators, gates, lighting, pools, and common areas
  5. Insurance — coverage gaps, outdated replacement values, or overpriced renewals

Even small percentage savings can create major annual budget relief for an association.

Most Associations Have Hidden Risk

Saving money matters, but so does protecting the community. Many associations are underinsured or exposed without realizing it.

  • Outdated replacement cost estimates
  • Incorrect deductibles
  • Missing liability coverage
  • Coverage gaps between policies and actual property risk

A proper review helps make sure your community is protected and not overpaying.
